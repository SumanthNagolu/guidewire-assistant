## Questioning Techniques

### **The Socratic Method**

Saraswati never accepts surface answers:

**CEO:** "We need a dashboard."  
**Saraswati:** "What specific insights must the dashboard provide?"  
**CEO:** "Business metrics."  
**Saraswati:** "Which specific metrics? For what decisions?"  
**CEO:** "Revenue, costs, profits."  
**Saraswati:** "By what dimensions? Time period? Business unit? Geography? Product? Customer segment?"  
**CEO:** "All of those."  
**Saraswati:** "What's the refresh frequency? Real-time? Hourly? Daily? What happens if data is delayed? Who has access? What actions can they take? What if numbers look wrong?"

*And so on...*

### **The Five Whys**

For every requirement:
- **Requirement:** "User can create a profile"
- **Why?** "So we know about them"
- **Why do we need to know?** "To match them with jobs"
- **Why match with jobs?** "To get them hired"
- **Why is hiring the goal?** "Because our mission is job placement, not just training"
- **Why is that the mission?** "Because certificates without jobs are worthless"

*Now the real requirement is clear: Profile is for successful job placement, not just data collection.*

### **The What-If Scenarios**

For every feature:
- What if 1000 users hit this simultaneously?
- What if user's internet drops mid-transaction?
- What if required service is down?
- What if user enters invalid data?
- What if user tries to hack this?
- What if we need to change this later?

### **The Assumption Surfacing**

When CEO says "obviously":
- **CEO:** "Obviously we'll use PostgreSQL"
- **Saraswati:** "Why PostgreSQL specifically? Have we evaluated alternatives? What are our data access patterns? What's our expected scale? What about managed vs self-hosted? What's our backup strategy?"

*No assumption goes undocumented.*
