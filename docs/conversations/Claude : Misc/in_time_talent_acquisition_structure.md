# InTime Talent Acquisition Organization & Process Blueprint (Integrated Lead Generation & Marketing Pod)

## I. Vision Alignment

Talent Acquisition is the **frontline engine** driving all three InTime pillars — **Recruiting**, **Bench Sales**, and **Training**. Its role is to generate, qualify, and nurture every inbound and outbound opportunity that fuels placements, enrollments, and client relationships.  

**Mission:** Build and sustain a continuous inflow of high-quality leads for training, staffing, and bench operations through a unified, data-driven, and brand-consistent process that maximizes efficiency and impact.

**Strategic Goals:**
- Establish one central lead-generation function serving all divisions.
- Build seamless coordination between lead generation, marketing, and talent operations.
- Use data intelligence to target high-value vendors, candidates, and training prospects.
- Transform every recruiter conversation into a potential lead source.
- Maintain a transparent reporting system that tracks every lead from discovery to conversion.

---

## II. Organizational Structure (Day 1 → Scalable Model)

### 1. Leadership Layer — **Senior Talent Acquisition Manager (STAM)**
- Leads the Talent Acquisition Pod.
- Owns the entire talent pipeline across Training, Recruiting, and Bench Sales.
- Defines lead-generation strategy, campaigns, and priorities.
- Oversees marketing (digital + social), internal hiring, and content creation.
- Builds and manages relationships with external vendors and universities.
- Works with pod leaders to ensure leads move to relevant delivery teams.
- Tracks KPIs and reports progress to the CEO weekly.

### 2. Senior Execution Layer — **Talent Acquisition Specialists / Lead Generation Executives**
- Execute daily lead-generation and marketing activities.
- Manage data sourcing, vendor contact, and outreach automation.
- Qualify leads using scoring frameworks (interest, readiness, fit).
- Maintain lead databases segmented by type (training, bench, client, vendor).
- Support internal hiring and SME (Subject Matter Expert) acquisition.

### 3. Marketing & Operations Layer — **Digital Marketing Executive / HR Coordinator**
- Manage all digital, social, and email marketing campaigns.
- Create and distribute content aligned with InTime’s brand tone.
- Handle onboarding for new internal hires and trainers.
- Support HR operations (offer letters, agreements, and compliance).
- Maintain alignment with all pods for unified brand and message consistency.

## III. Process Flow — The Integrated Lead Engine

> **Objective:** Ensure that every lead, from training inquiries to vendor partnerships, is captured, qualified, and routed efficiently to maximize conversion.

### Step 1: **Lead Discovery & Capture**
- Collect leads from:
  - LinkedIn and social media campaigns.
  - Training demo sign-ups and webinar registrations.
  - Vendor databases and partner networks.
  - Recruiter conversations and referrals.
  - Inbound web inquiries and digital ads.
- Log all leads in the **Talent Pipeline Board** with source tags and status.

### Step 2: **Qualification & Categorization**
- Use standard qualification checklist:
  - For Training: Interest, skill focus, timeline, financial readiness.
  - For Bench: Vendor credibility, requirement volume, payment reliability.
  - For Recruiting: Hiring authority, recurring requirement pattern, industry fit.
- Assign Lead Stage: New → Contacted → Qualified → Converted → Nurture.

### Step 3: **Outreach & Engagement**
- Execute coordinated outreach campaigns:
  - Training: Demo invitations, success stories, course outcomes.
  - Recruiting: Capability decks, client references, rate sheets.
  - Bench: Consultant hotlists, talent pool showcases, resume bundles.
- Follow-up via email + WhatsApp + LinkedIn within 24 hours.
- Escalate qualified leads to relevant delivery pods.

### Step 4: **Handoff to Delivery Pods**
- Route leads to:
  - **Recruiting Pod:** Clients and hiring partners.
  - **Bench Pod:** Vendor contacts and requirement submissions.
  - **Training Pod:** Students and trainer leads.
- Maintain visibility through Monday.com automations and tagging.

### Step 5: **Follow-Up & Conversion Tracking**
- Monitor conversion until:
  - Training → Enrollment complete.
  - Recruiting → Requirement submission or MSA signed.
  - Bench → Requirement received or submission done.
- Record conversion metrics daily.
- Weekly review by STAM with all pod heads.

## VI. Monday.com Boards & Ecosystem

1. **Talent Pipeline Board:** Source, Category, Lead Owner, Status, Stage, Next Action, Conversion Value.
2. **Lead Qualification Board:** Checklist-driven system for evaluating readiness and routing.
3. **Marketing Campaigns Board:** Tracks ongoing email, social, and ad campaigns.
4. **Hiring & HR Board:** Tracks internal recruitment and onboarding progress.
5. **Dashboard Board:** Consolidates daily, weekly, and monthly lead metrics for leadership.

**Automation Highlights:**
- New lead → Auto-assign to TA Specialist.
- Qualified lead → Auto-route to relevant pod.
- Conversion → Notify STAM and CEO.

## VII. Quality Control & Reporting Framework

**Weekly Quality Audits:**
- Verify all leads have accurate contact info, source tags, and status updates.
- Ensure leads are contacted within 24 hours.
- Check lead notes for clarity and professionalism.
- Review marketing content for brand tone and compliance.

**Monthly Performance Review:**
| Metric | Target | Owner |
|--------|---------|-------|
